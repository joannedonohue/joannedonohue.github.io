Mars Petcare

The Problem

No benchmarks, no consistent measurement, no way to know if marketing creative was actually working. Sales teams toggled between a dozen disconnected tools to answer basic business questions — figuring out whether Greenies Dental Chews had a bad week required pulling three separate reports and doing the math by hand.

The eCommerce organization was flying blind. Decisions about creative spend, promotional timing, and content optimization were based on gut feel rather than data. And with brands like Greenies, Nutro, and Royal Canin all sharing the same fragmented infrastructure, there was no single source of truth.

The Approach

Track 1 — Analytics Infrastructure

Led a cross-functional tiger team to consolidate reporting. Interviewed sales teams about real decisions and pain points. Designed a structured evaluation framework across five shortlisted eCommerce analytics platforms. Selected Stackline and worked directly with their engineering team on integration. Ran interactive training sessions to drive adoption and overcome Excel muscle memory.

Track 2 — Test-and-Learn Roadmap

Developed the first structured testing roadmap Mars eCommerce had ever had — clear milestones and measurable hypotheses for the marketing agency. Early tests established baselines for each marketing lever: search, display, and promotions. Later tests built on those learnings, progressively tightening targeting and timing.

The critical insight: heavy promotional advertising consistently underperformed always-on retargeting. That single finding, backed by rigorous incrementality analysis, redirected millions in spend.

The Outcome

The budget shift from heavy promotional to always-on retargeting drove millions in incremental sales. But the longer-lasting impact was cultural: for the first time, the Mars eCommerce organization had a shared language for evaluating what was working, a system for learning from what wasn't, and the confidence to make bold spend decisions backed by data.
